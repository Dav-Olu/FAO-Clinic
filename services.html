Answers to Your Frequency Asked Questions

FAO Dental Clinic provides a full range of dental services including routine cleanings, fillings, root canals, crowns, bridges, teeth whitening, orthodontics (braces and aligners), and dental implants. They also offer preventive care and pediatric dentistry for children.

It’s recommended to visit the dentist every six months for a routine check-up and cleaning. However, patients with gum disease, braces, or other ongoing treatments may need more frequent visits.

If you experience a dental emergency such as a knocked-out tooth, severe toothache, or broken dental work, contact FAO Dental Clinic immediately. They offer emergency appointments and will guide you on what to do until you arrive.

Yes, FAO Dental Clinic accepts most major dental insurance plans. For patients without insurance, they offer flexible payment options and financing plans to make dental care accessible and affordable.

Yes, professional teeth whitening at FAO Dental Clinic is safe, effective, and supervised by dental professionals. It provides faster and more noticeable results compared to over-the-counter products, with minimal sensitivity.

Yes, FAO Dental Clinic offers comprehensive pediatric dental care. Their friendly team is experienced in working with children of all ages, making visits fun and stress-free while focusing on early prevention, education, and gentle treatment.

Booking an appointment is easy! You can call the clinic directly, visit their website to schedule online, or walk in to check availability. For faster service, it’s best to book ahead—especially for weekend or evening slots.
